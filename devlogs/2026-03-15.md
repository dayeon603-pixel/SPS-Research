**Finding:** Theorem 1 (Family Monotonicity) requires no axioms from A1–A5 at all. I had been listing A3 as a requirement (measurability needed to compare expectations) but A3 is only needed if the expectations exist as Lebesgue integrals. Theorem 1 is a purely pointwise result: $\mathcal{T}_1 \subseteq \mathcal{T}_2 \Rightarrow \mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T}_1,\varepsilon} \leq \mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T}_2,\varepsilon}$ (at each $x$, taking sup over larger set doesn't decrease). The expectation step follows from the pointwise inequality + linearity of expectation, not from measurability of the sensitivity map. Removing A3 from Theorem 1's prerequisites.

**Finding:** A5.2 (Semantic preservation) is used in the framework conceptually, but no theorem invokes it in its proof. A5.2 is the conceptual motivation for restricting to $\mathcal{T}$, not a technical assumption used in any proof.

This is fine — A5.2 is a modeling assumption that characterizes which families $\mathcal{T}$ are meaningful to use. It's not a mathematical requirement on the proofs. But I should note this explicitly so readers don't wonder why it never appears in proof boxes.

A5.3 (Composition): `EmbeddingPerturbationFamily` satisfies composition because $T_{\alpha_1}^{v_1} \circ T_{\alpha_2}^{v_2}(E) = E + \alpha_1 v_1 + \alpha_2 v_2$. If $v_1 + v_2$ (normalized) is in $A_x$, the composition is in the family. For synonym-direction families, this is approximately satisfied if the synonym directions are drawn from the same vocabulary position.

**What each number means:**

- $\bar{\gamma}$: fraction of Jacobian sensitivity in non-semantic directions (higher = better geometry)
