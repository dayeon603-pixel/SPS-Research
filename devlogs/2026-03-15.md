**Finalized axiom list in `theory/definitions.md`:**

- **A1 (Fréchet Differentiability):** $f_\theta$ is Fréchet differentiable at every $x \in \mathrm{supp}(\mathcal{D})$. Required by: Theorem 2 (both parts), Corollary 1.

- **A2 (Transformation Compactness):** $A_x^{(\varepsilon)}$ is compact in $\mathbb{S}^{d-1}$. Required by: Theorem 2 Part (ii), Corollary 1. This is what allows the limit $\varepsilon \to 0$ and sup over $A_x$ to be interchanged.

- **A3 (Measurability):** $x \mapsto \mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T},\varepsilon}(f_\theta; x)$ is $\mathcal{D}$-measurable. Required by: Proposition 1 (expectation must be defined), Definition 2 (the expectation must exist as a Lebesgue integral).

- **A4 (Integrability):** $\mathbb{E}_{x \sim \mathcal{D}}[\mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T},\varepsilon}(f_\theta; x)] < \infty$. Required by: Proposition 1 (strict positivity $\mathrm{SPS} > 0$). Without A4, only $\mathrm{SPS} \geq 0$.

- **A5 (Transformation Family Axioms):**
  - A5.1 (Identity): $\mathrm{Id} \in \mathcal{T}$, $c(\mathrm{Id}, x) = 0$.
  - A5.2 (Semantic preservation): every $T \in \mathcal{T}$ preserves task-relevant labels.
  - A5.3 (Closure under composition): if $T_1, T_2 \in \mathcal{T}$, then $T_2 \circ T_1 \in \mathcal{T}$.

Note: A5.2 is the hardest to verify formally. For the operational definition (curated families), it's assumed by construction. A5.3 is used only by Theorem 4 — and Theorem 4 also requires the explicit chain constraint that A5.3 alone doesn't provide.

**Checking internal consistency of A5:**

A5.1 (Identity in $\mathcal{T}$) is satisfied by the `EmbeddingPerturbationFamily` — the case $\alpha = 0$ (or rather, the limit as $\alpha \to 0$) gives the identity transformation. In the code, the lower bound on $\alpha$ is $0$ (exclusive) so identity is never sampled. Consistent.

A5.3 (composition closure) is satisfied for `EmbeddingPerturbationFamily`: if $T_1 = T_{\alpha_1}^{v_1}$ and $T_2 = T_{\alpha_2}^{v_2}$, then $T_2 \circ T_1(E) = E + \alpha_1 v_1 + \alpha_2 v_2$. This is also an element of $\mathcal{T}_{\text{emb}}$ if $v_1 + v_2 \cdot (\alpha_2/\alpha_1)$ is in the convex hull of $A_x$ — which requires the family to be closed under linear combinations of directions. The current implementation only generates single-direction perturbations, so the composition is in the family if the combined direction is in $A_x$. This is approximate for the synonym-direction family.

A5.3 is harder to verify for `SynonymSubstitutionFamily`: composing two synonym substitutions gives a sequence with two or more substitutions. The code handles this via `max_substitutions` — setting it to 2 or 3 is equivalent to allowing limited composition.

### Lab Notes

The most interesting finding today: Theorem 1 requires no assumptions. The monotonicity of SPS with respect to expansion of $\mathcal{T}$ is a pure consequence of the supremum being monotone. This means that even for completely pathological transformation families (not satisfying any of A1–A5), the relative ordering of SPS values is preserved as you expand $\mathcal{T}$. That's a useful property — it means comparisons like "SPS under $\mathcal{T}_{\text{syn}} \geq$ SPS under $\mathcal{T}_{\text{back}}$" don't require any regularity assumptions.

The dependency diagram makes it clear that A3 and A4 are only needed for Proposition 1 and the basic well-definedness claims. The actual interesting results (Theorems 2–4, Corollary 1) all rely on geometric properties (A1, A2) or algebraic structure (A5). The measurability and integrability are just housekeeping.

The five-axiom structure is cleaner than what I had at the start of this project. The first two weeks were working with no formal axioms at all — just informal "semantic-preserving transformation." Having the axioms explicit now makes it clear what assumptions are load-bearing.

### Hypotheses

1. **A2 (compactness) is the most model-dependent axiom.** Whether $A_x^{(\varepsilon)}$ is compact depends on the transformation family and the input distribution. For finite synonym dictionaries, $A_x$ is a finite set (compact trivially). For back-translation (continuous family), compactness is less obvious. Theorem 2 Part (ii) requires compactness — this limits its applicability to discrete transformation families unless continuity of the transformation is established.

2. **A5.3 (composition closure) can be weakened to "closure at scale $\varepsilon/2$" for Theorem 4.** The chain constraint in Theorem 4 is already $c(T_1, x) \leq \varepsilon$ and $c(T_2, T_1 x) \leq \varepsilon$ separately. A5.3 is used to ensure $T_2 \circ T_1 \in \mathcal{T}$, but a weaker version — "if $c(T_1, x), c(T_2, T_1 x) \leq \varepsilon/2$ then $T_2 \circ T_1 \in \mathcal{T}$ with $c \leq \varepsilon$" — would suffice.
