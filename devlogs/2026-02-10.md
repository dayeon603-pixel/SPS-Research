# Dev Log — 2026-02-10: First Experiment — Random Perturbation Testing (Failed)

## Goal
Test model robustness by adding Gaussian noise to embeddings and measuring output consistency.

## What Happened
- Added random noise to intermediate embeddings, measured prediction stability
- Outputs appeared super stable — nearly interpreted this as "model is robust"
- Realized I was measuring **softmax saturation**, not representation quality
- At high confidence, softmax is nearly flat — you can push logits around without changing predictions
- The noise had no semantic grounding — disrupts syntax, morphology, and meaning simultaneously

## Root Cause
Random perturbations have no semantic structure. Measuring stability under meaningless noise tells you nothing about whether the model understands meaning. A model that memorizes training data looks perfectly "robust" under this metric.

## Decision
Move toward **constrained transformation families** where perturbations have known semantic properties (paraphrases, syntax transforms, translations). If the perturbation has no semantic meaning, neither does the stability measurement.

## Impact
This is the founding insight of the SPS framework: perturbations must be structured to be informative.
