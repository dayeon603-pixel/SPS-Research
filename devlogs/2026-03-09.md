## [12:00 UTC] Whiteboard Session — Theorem 4 Proof Attempts + Finding the Chain Constraint

### State at Session Start

Reading done. Now attempt the Theorem 4 proof.

**Original statement (from notes):**

> Under A5.3, for $T_1, T_2 \in \mathcal{T}$ with $c(T_1, x) \leq \varepsilon$:
> $$d_\mathcal{Y}(f(x), f(T_2 T_1 x)) \leq \mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T},\varepsilon}(f; x) \cdot c(T_1, x) + \mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T},\varepsilon}(f; T_1 x) \cdot c(T_2, T_1 x).$$

### Work Completed

**Proof attempt — first try:**

Step 1: Triangle inequality in $(\mathcal{Y}, d_\mathcal{Y})$:

$$d_\mathcal{Y}(f(x), f(T_2 T_1 x)) \leq d_\mathcal{Y}(f(x), f(T_1 x)) + d_\mathcal{Y}(f(T_1 x), f(T_2 T_1 x)).$$

Step 2: First term. $T_1 \in \mathcal{T}$, $c(T_1, x) \leq \varepsilon$ (hypothesis). By Definition 1:

$$d_\mathcal{Y}(f(x), f(T_1 x)) \leq \mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T},\varepsilon}(f; x) \cdot c(T_1, x). \quad \checkmark$$

Step 3: Second term. Want to bound $d_\mathcal{Y}(f(T_1 x), f(T_2 T_1 x))$ using $\mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T},\varepsilon}(f; T_1 x)$.

By Definition 1 with basepoint $T_1 x$:

$$d_\mathcal{Y}(f(T_1 x), f(T_2 T_1 x)) \leq \mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T},\varepsilon}(f; T_1 x) \cdot c(T_2, T_1 x)$$

**only if $c(T_2, T_1 x) \leq \varepsilon$.**

But the hypothesis only states $c(T_1, x) \leq \varepsilon$ — nothing about $c(T_2, T_1 x)$. The second transformation's cost is measured at the intermediate basepoint $T_1 x$, not at $x$. These can differ.

**Counter-example showing the constraint is necessary:**

Let $T_1$ move $x$ by $\varepsilon/2$ in direction $v_1$. Now $T_1 x = x + (\varepsilon/2) v_1$.

Let $T_2$ move the basepoint by $2\varepsilon$ (large step). $c(T_2, T_1 x) = 2\varepsilon > \varepsilon$.

The sensitivity $\mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T},\varepsilon}(f; T_1 x)$ is defined only for transformations within radius $\varepsilon$ of $T_1 x$. $T_2$ lies outside this radius. So $\mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T},\varepsilon}(f; T_1 x) \cdot c(T_2, T_1 x)$ doesn't bound the second term when $T_2$ is large.

The original theorem statement is wrong — it's asserting the bound holds for any $T_2$, but the bound uses the local sensitivity at $T_1 x$ which is only valid for $T_2$ within $\varepsilon$ of $T_1 x$.

**The fix:**

Add the constraint $c(T_2, T_1 x) \leq \varepsilon$ explicitly to the theorem hypothesis. The corrected theorem:

> Under A5.3, let $T_1, T_2 \in \mathcal{T}$ with $c(T_1, x) \leq \varepsilon$ **and $c(T_2, T_1 x) \leq \varepsilon$**. Then:
> $$d_\mathcal{Y}(f(x), f(T_2 T_1 x)) \leq \mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T},\varepsilon}(f; x) \cdot c(T_1, x) + \mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T},\varepsilon}(f; T_1 x) \cdot c(T_2, T_1 x).$$

With both constraints, the proof goes through cleanly (each sensitivity bound applies at its respective basepoint, and each transformation is within $\varepsilon$).

**Why A5.3 alone doesn't imply the chain constraint:**

A5.3 (closure under composition): if $T_1, T_2 \in \mathcal{T}$, then $T_2 \circ T_1 \in \mathcal{T}$.

This says the composition is in the family. It says nothing about $c(T_2, T_1 x)$. A transformation can be in $\mathcal{T}$ but have very large cost at a particular point.

A sufficient condition for $c(T_2, T_1 x) \leq \varepsilon$: require $c(T_1, x) \leq \varepsilon/2$ and $c(T_2, T_1 x) \leq \varepsilon/2$. Then by triangle inequality for $c$ (if $c$ satisfies the triangle inequality, which depends on the family), the composition $T_2 \circ T_1$ has cost $\leq \varepsilon$. But this is a stronger hypothesis that restricts both transformations to half the radius.

For the theorem statement, the cleanest formulation is to require $c(T_2, T_1 x) \leq \varepsilon$ explicitly and note that A5 + small $\varepsilon$ makes this approximately automatic.

**Updated `theory/definitions.md`:**

Added to A5 remark:

> "The constraint $c(T_2, T_1 x) \leq \varepsilon$ in Theorem 4 is not automatic from $c(T_1, x) \leq \varepsilon$ and A5.3 alone. It must be imposed explicitly. A sufficient condition is $c(T_1, x) \leq \varepsilon/2$ and $c(T_2, T_1 x) \leq \varepsilon/2$."

Committed as `fix(theory): theorem-4 add chain constraint c(T2, T1x) <= eps`.

**Geometric interpretation:**

$\varepsilon$ defines a local neighborhood. After $T_1$, we're at $T_1 x$ which might be $\varepsilon$-far from $x$. The sensitivity $\mathrm{Sens}(f; T_1 x)$ is defined over transformations within $\varepsilon$ of $T_1 x$. If $T_2$ exceeds this radius when applied at $T_1 x$, it leaves the domain where the local bound applies.

This is a locality constraint on the theorem — it's a local result, valid for small $\varepsilon$.
