### Work Completed

**Attempted to write Theorem 2.**

Original statement I had sketched on Feb 27:

> **Theorem 2.** Under A1, let $\delta(x) := \lim_{\alpha \to 0} \|f(x + \alpha \delta(x)) - f(x)\| / \alpha$. Then:
>
> $$\delta(x) = \|J_f(x)\|_{A_x}$$

Tried to write the proof. Got stuck immediately.

The left-hand side $\delta(x)$ is the directional derivative norm for a single fixed perturbation direction. The right-hand side $\|J_f(x)\|_{A_x}$ is the supremum over all directions in $A_x$. These are not equal in general.

The equality $\delta(x) = \|J_f(x)\|_{A_x}$ would require the supremum to be achieved exactly at the direction corresponding to $\delta(x)$. That's only guaranteed if $A_x$ is a singleton — one direction — which it's not. If there exists any $v \in A_x$ with $\|J_f(x)\, v\| > \|J_f(x)\, \delta(x)/\|\delta(x)\|\|$, then the RHS is strictly larger than the LHS.

I was conflating a single directional derivative with the restricted operator norm.

**The fix: split into two claims.**

Revised Theorem 2 (two-part):

*Part (i): Single direction.* For each fixed $v \in A_x$ with $\|v\| = 1$:

$$\lim_{\alpha \to 0} \frac{\|f(x + \alpha v) - f(x)\|}{\alpha} = \|J_f(x)\, v\|$$

This is fine and provable directly from the Fréchet derivative definition.

*Part (ii): Restricted operator norm.* Under A1 and A2 (compactness of $A_x^{(\varepsilon)}$):

$$\lim_{\varepsilon \to 0}\, \mathrm{Sens}_{\mathcal{T},\varepsilon}(f; x) = \sup_{v \in A_x, \|v\|=1} \|J_f(x)\, v\| = \|J_f(x)\|_{A_x}$$

This requires more: the limit and sup need to be interchangeable, which follows from compactness (A2) and uniform convergence of the Fréchet remainder on compact sets.

The original single equation $\delta(x) = \|J_f(x)\|_{A_x}$ was only asserting equality, not $\leq$. The corrected Part (i) gives $\delta(x) \leq \|J_f(x)\|_{A_x}$ (since $\delta(x)$ is one term in the sup). Part (ii) is the actual useful result.

### Lab Notes

This is the kind of error that's easy to make in analysis and hard to catch without writing up the proof carefully. A single JVP gives you the sensitivity along one direction. The sup over directions is a categorically different object — it's a property of the operator, not a property of a single evaluation.

I think the original confusion came from writing the empirical estimator first (which happens to compute the max over $m$ transformations, getting the sup approximately right) and then writing the theorem to match what I thought the code was computing. If I had written the theorem first, I would have been forced to be precise about which quantity I was claiming equality for.

Also: the underestimation matters for the spectral gap. $\bar{\gamma}$ with the corrected restricted norm:

$$\bar{\gamma}_{\text{corrected}} = 1 - 0.351 / 0.847 = 0.586$$

$$\bar{\gamma}_{\text{incorrect}} = 1 - 0.284 / 0.847 = 0.665$$

The true gap is 0.586, not 0.665. A difference of 0.079 in $\bar{\gamma}$ is meaningful — it changes the interpretation of how much of the Jacobian's sensitivity is aligned with semantic directions.

### Hypotheses

1. **The underestimation from single-direction vs restricted norm scales with $K$.** As $K$ increases, the max over $K$ directions will increasingly approach the true sup over $A_x$. The bias in the original single-direction estimate should vanish as $K \to \infty$.

2. **Restricted norm estimates with $K=8$ are approximately correct for synonym-substitution families.** Most tokens have 3–5 meaningful synonyms. At $K=8$ directions, most of the semantic direction space is covered. Diminishing returns should be observed beyond $K=16$.

3. **The two-part structure of Theorem 2 is necessary to connect the finite-$\varepsilon$ empirical estimator to the Jacobian.** Part (i) alone only gives one direction. Part (ii) with A2 (compactness) is what makes the $\varepsilon \to 0$ limit recover the full restricted norm.
