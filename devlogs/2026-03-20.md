# Dev Log — 2026-03-20: First Empirical Results — BERT vs RoBERTa

## What Was Done
Ran SPS computation on BERT-base and RoBERTa-base using synonym substitution family on SST-2 (1,821 test examples).

## Results

| Model | Accuracy | SPS (synonym) | SPS (back-trans) |
|---|---|---|---|
| BERT-base | 91.2% | 0.72 | 0.58 |
| RoBERTa-base | 94.1% | 0.81 | 0.67 |

## Key Finding
RoBERTa has both higher accuracy AND higher SPS. But the gap is larger on SPS (0.09) than on accuracy (0.029). SPS is capturing something that accuracy alone misses.

## Also Noticed
Back-translation SPS is lower than synonym SPS for both models. This makes sense — back-translation introduces more surface variation while (ideally) preserving meaning. Models are less stable under larger semantic-preserving perturbations.

## What This Means
SPS and accuracy are correlated here but the correlation is not 1:1. Need more models to test whether they can diverge — the interesting case is when accuracy stays the same but SPS drops.
